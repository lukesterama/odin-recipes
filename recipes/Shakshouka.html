Shakshouka

Description:

I would like to introduce you to one of my favorite breakfast dishes, Shakshouka! I say breakfast but truthfully Shakshouka can be eaten anytime of the day.

Shakshouka or shakshuka is a delicious combination of eggs poached in a sauce of tomatoes, olive oil, peppers, onions and garlic, and commonly spiced with cumin, paprika and cayenne pepper.

The word Shakshouka loosely translates to all mixed up in Arabic, which is an apt description and hints at the potent spice and exotic flavors of this remarkably simple yet delicious dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1.25 cups chopped onions
  • 1 cup thinly sliced bell peppers, any color
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2.5 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 hot chile pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • salt, to taste

Steps:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Stir in the onion, bell peppers, and garlic; cook and stir until the vegetables have softened and the onion has turned translucent, about 5 minutes
  3. Combine the tomatoes, cumin, paprika, salt, and chile pepper into a bowl and mix briefly.
  4. Pour the tomato mixture into the skillet cast iron's work best, stir to combine all ingredients, and let simmer, uncovered, until the tomato juices have cooked off, about 10 minutes.
  5. Make four indentations in the tomato mixture with the back of a spoon or ladle and crack the eggs into each indentation.
  6. Cover the skillet and let the eggs cook until they're firm, 3-5 minutes.
  7. Take the skillet off the heat, add salt and pepper to taste, serve and enjoy!
